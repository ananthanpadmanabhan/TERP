Who We Are

The Earth Reimagined Project (TERP) is a sustainability initiative rooted in Kerala, India, a region where ecological fragility and cultural resilience intersect. Headquartered in Trivandrum, TERP operates across both urban and rural landscapes of Kerala, applying localized climate action models to real-world systems.

Our work spans sustainability assessments, community engagement, and responsible tourism, all grounded in the belief that climate action must be data-informed, context-specific, and socially inclusive.

Our Focus Areas

  • Sustainability Assessments – Waste, energy, and water audits using data-driven tools
  • Community Engagement – Climate literacy, public dialogues, and creative campaigns
  • Responsible Tourism – Promoting local, women-led, and tribal enterprises through curated travel initiatives

Our Approach

  • Data-Driven: We build frameworks to quantify environmental impact and track progress
  • Inclusive: Programs are designed with local partners, prioritizing grassroots participation
  • Scalable: Our models are rooted in Kerala but adaptable to similar regions like the Nilgiris

Sister Organization: AAHAS Foundation

AAHAS focuses on education, arts, and social inclusion. It collaborates closely with TERP on initiatives like:

  • School-based climate residencies
  • University dialogue series
  • Cultural sustainability projects

What Guides Us

We believe sustainability is not a luxury — it’s a shared responsibility rooted in community, creativity, and care. Real change happens when people feel connected to the systems they’re part of.

What Drives Us

We work to make sustainability more accessible, measurable, and meaningful through audits, creative engagement, and collaborative tools, moving forward with clarity, culture, and purpose.

Our Journey: A Quiet Revolt

TERP began with a simple question: "Why are we still discussing sustainability in such disconnected ways?" It was born from the frustration of seeing policies that looked good on paper but felt hollow on the ground. We started small, proving that sustainability could be playful, local, measurable, and collective.

Our Foundational Tool

Our Sustainability SWOT Analysis provides a 360-degree view, empowering organizations to act with confidence.

Strengths

Existing sustainable practices, passionate team members, community trust.

Weaknesses

High energy consumption, reliance on single-use plastics, lack of data tracking.

Opportunities

Green grants, local sourcing partnerships, engaging a wider audience online.

Threats

Regulatory changes, supply chain disruptions, stakeholder skepticism.
