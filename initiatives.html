Koottam Sustainability Assessment

Technical Sustainability Tools

Summary
Conducted at the 2025 Koottam Festival in Trivandrum, this assessment involved a detailed sustainability audit focusing on waste, energy, and transportation impacts. Using European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), TERP developed practical, low-carbon recommendations for future event planning.
Relevance
Showcases TERP’s ability to apply technical assessment frameworks in live, large-scale public contexts.
Key Highlights
  • 3-day on-ground observation, 6,000+ footfall
  • Identified diesel generator inefficiencies and waste segregation challenges
  • Actionable roadmap submitted to organizers

Roots & Routes

Sustainable Travel Planning Initiative

Summary
Roots & Routes is a flagship initiative by TERP designed to reimagine tourism as a low-impact, community-driven experience. Through a map-based digital interface, the tool helps travelers generate custom itineraries that include not just scenic destinations, but verified, women-led and local enterprises — from tribal craft hubs to community cafés.
Relevance
Roots & Routes can be easily adapted to the Nilgiris context to promote eco-cultural tourism, support indigenous livelihoods, and highlight forest-based micro-enterprises in a curated, visitor-friendly format.
Key Highlights
  • Lightweight, mobile-first web tool
  • Highlights experiential stops like homestays, crafts, and forest-based services
  • Combines storytelling, geographic mapping, and micro-enterprise visibility
  • Built in collaboration with student teams and grassroots networks

Unmute

Youth & Sustainability Dialogue Series

Summary
This campus-based dialogue series invited students and experts to openly discuss pressing issues — from mental health to sustainable fashion. The events were held in Trivandrum and co-designed with youth participants.
Relevance
Enables peer-to-peer sustainability literacy and low-cost, high-impact engagement with university communities.
Key Highlights
  • Student moderation + participation
  • Formats include open floor + panel mix
  • Strong interest from academic institutions

Chayakkodu

Tribal Arts & Education Residency

Summary
A mural art and environmental education residency held at a tribal LP school in Kannur district. Through art, students explored themes of forest, identity, and ecological relationships, leaving behind a permanent visual narrative on their school wall.
Relevance
Bridges sustainability with cultural preservation and participatory education — a model that can be scaled across rural schools in ecologically sensitive districts.
Key Highlights
  • Worked with tribal educators and artists
  • Thematic sessions on nature, kinship, and climate
  • Expected Output: a wall-sized mural + oral storytelling project
